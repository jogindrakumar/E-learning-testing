About E-Learning Activity

E-Learning is a learning system based on formalised teaching with the help of electronic resources such as computers, tablets and cellular phones connected to the internet. It makes easy for users to learn anytime and anywhere. E-Learning contains visuals, apart from holding the attention of the student, are also retained by the brain for longer periods. E-Learning also enables educators to get a higher degree of coverage to communicate the message in a consistent way for their students. A learning management system (LMS) is a software application that is used to deliver E- Learning. Instead of having the E-Learning content spread out over different hard drives and devices, LMS allows to store all the E-Learning materials in one location. Delivering the course through an LMS keeps the content centralized so that all the students have a single source of content, instructions, and questions. LMS provides endless opportunities for learning through apps, smartphones, videos, search engines, and other real-time information sources.

User Roles

CONTENT CREATOR

The content creator of a Course in UG courses and of a unit in PG courses is responsible for creating the E-Content lecture wise, by following the content structure and format guidelines available on E-Learning Portal. Each Lecture will be maximum of one hour.

UNIT REVIEWER

For each unit under a course, there is one Unit Reviewer , The role of the reviewer is to review the content created by the content creator for a particular unit.

COURSE REVIEWER

The role of course reviewer is to review all the content submitted by Unit Reviewer/Content Creator for a particular course and ensures the quality and delivery of the E-Course.

NODAL OFFICER

Nodal officer Verifies and authenticates the teaching experience and other details submitted by the faculty of the university at the time of application submission and coordinate with the faculty in timely completion of the E-Courses from the university.

Background

The Project "Investments in ICAR Leadership in Agricultural Higher Education" is a Component-2 NAHEP project funded by the world bank. Vision of the project is strengthening of agricultural education system in India. The lead institute for the project is ICAR-IASRI and the collaborating institutes are ICAR-NIAP and ICAR-NAARM.

The major objectives of the project at ICAR-IASRI are as follows:

To develop the ICT infrastructure as technical assistance to Agricultural Universities

To develop digital information systems for agricultural education, data collection, analysis, and dissemination

To develop and implement next-generation management systems covering information regarding procurement to contract management and financial management areas


Under the first objective, establishment of E-Learning/M-Learning systems for participating AUs is a major activity.


The main objectives of E-Learning activity are:

Revision of existing UG e-courses .

Development of e-courses for master’s and PhD courses.

Deployment of e-courses and study on E-Learning for inclusiveness of all students.
